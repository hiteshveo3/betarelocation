Man and van London cost: quick answer

For most small London moves, the starting price is £45/hour for one mover and a van. If you need heavier lifting, stairs, bulky furniture, or faster loading, two movers and a van from £65/hour is usually the better choice.

The lowest final price is usually for a short single-item job with easy parking and ground-floor access. The price increases when the team needs more time, more people, specialist handling, or extra waiting time.

What affects the final moving cost?

The biggest cost factor is time. A move with easy parking, lift access, packed boxes, and a short drive will usually cost less than a move with stairs, waiting time, awkward furniture, or long walking distance from van to door.

  • Distance between pickup and drop-off.
  • Number and size of items.
  • Stairs, lifts, parking, and building access.
  • Packing, dismantling, or furniture protection.
  • Same-day urgency or limited time windows.
  • Specialist items such as pianos, art, antiques, or hoisting.

Access matters

Short walking distance from van to door can save more time than a cheaper hourly rate.

Packing matters

Loose items slow loading. Boxed and labelled items make the move cleaner and faster.

Team size matters

Two movers often cost less overall when stairs, sofas, wardrobes, or heavy items are involved.

What is included in the price?

A standard Beta Relocation man and van quote includes the mover or movers, the van, loading, transport, unloading, and Goods in Transit insurance up to GBP10,000. Fuel and mileage are included inside London quotes unless your move has unusual distance or access requirements.

When do you need a custom quote?

You should request a custom quote for pianos, antiques, artwork, aquariums, hoisting, large office equipment, very high-value items, or access that requires extra planning.

How to keep your move cheaper

  • Pack boxes before the movers arrive.
  • Reserve parking close to the entrance where possible.
  • Send photos of bulky items before booking.
  • Disassemble beds or large furniture in advance if you can.
  • Move on a flexible weekday if timing allows.
  • Choose two movers when heavy lifting would slow one person down.

One mover vs two movers: which is better?

One mover is usually fine for single items, boxes, small student moves, and light studio moves. Two movers are better for sofas, wardrobes, stairs, larger flats, faster loading, and safer handling. If the job involves heavy lifting, two movers can reduce total time and protect your items.
